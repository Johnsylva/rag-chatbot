# Classic Cocktail Guide

Welcome to the Classic Cocktail Guide! This guide covers the most popular cocktails, their history, ingredients, and preparation methods.

## Margarita

The Margarita is one of the most popular cocktails in the world, originating from Mexico in the 1930s or 1940s.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz tequila (blanco or reposado)
- 1 oz fresh lime juice
- 1 oz Cointreau or triple sec
- Salt for rimming (optional)
- Lime wheel for garnish

### Instructions
1. If desired, rim a rocks glass with salt by running a lime wedge around the edge and dipping in salt.
2. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
3. Add tequila, lime juice, and Cointreau.
4. Shake vigorously for 15-20 seconds until well chilled.
5. Strain into the prepared glass over fresh ice.
6. Garnish with a lime wheel.

### Variations
- **Frozen Margarita**: Blend all ingredients with ice until smooth.
- **Spicy Margarita**: Add muddled jalapeño slices.
- **Cadillac Margarita**: Use Grand Marnier instead of triple sec.

### Tips
- Always use fresh lime juice, never bottled.
- Quality tequila makes a significant difference.
- The ideal ratio is 2:1:1 (tequila:lime:orange liqueur).

## Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned is considered one of the original cocktails, dating back to the early 1800s. It's a simple but sophisticated drink that highlights the quality of the whiskey.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz bourbon or rye whiskey
- 1 sugar cube (or 1/2 oz simple syrup)
- 2-3 dashes Angostura bitters
- Orange peel for garnish
- Luxardo cherry for garnish (optional)

### Instructions
1. Place the sugar cube in a rocks glass.
2. Add the bitters directly onto the sugar cube.
3. Add a splash of water and muddle until the sugar dissolves.
4. Add a large ice cube or several smaller cubes.
5. Pour the whiskey over the ice.
6. Stir gently for 20-30 seconds.
7. Express the oil from an orange peel over the drink and drop it in.
8. Optionally add a Luxardo cherry.

### Variations
- **Oaxacan Old Fashioned**: Use mezcal instead of whiskey.
- **Rum Old Fashioned**: Use aged rum.
- **Maple Old Fashioned**: Use maple syrup instead of sugar.

### Tips
- Use a large ice cube to minimize dilution.
- Rye whiskey gives a spicier flavor, bourbon is sweeter.
- Don't over-muddle; you want to dissolve the sugar, not pulverize it.

## Mojito

The Mojito originated in Havana, Cuba and has become one of the most refreshing summer cocktails. Ernest Hemingway was famously a fan.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz white rum
- 1 oz fresh lime juice
- 2 teaspoons sugar
- 6-8 fresh mint leaves
- Soda water
- Mint sprig and lime wheel for garnish

### Instructions
1. Place mint leaves and sugar in a highball glass.
2. Gently muddle to release the mint oils (don't shred the leaves).
3. Add lime juice and stir to dissolve the sugar.
4. Fill the glass with ice.
5. Add rum and stir.
6. Top with soda water.
7. Garnish with a mint sprig and lime wheel.

### Variations
- **Strawberry Mojito**: Add muddled fresh strawberries.
- **Coconut Mojito**: Use coconut rum.
- **Watermelon Mojito**: Add fresh watermelon juice.

### Tips
- Muddle gently; bruising the mint too much creates a bitter taste.
- Use superfine sugar for easier dissolving.
- Spearmint is the traditional choice for mojitos.

## Moscow Mule

The Moscow Mule was invented in 1941 in Los Angeles and helped popularize vodka in America. It's traditionally served in a copper mug.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz vodka
- 4 oz ginger beer
- 1/2 oz fresh lime juice
- Lime wheel for garnish

### Instructions
1. Fill a copper mug with ice.
2. Add vodka and lime juice.
3. Top with ginger beer.
4. Stir gently to combine.
5. Garnish with a lime wheel.

### Variations
- **Kentucky Mule**: Use bourbon instead of vodka.
- **Mexican Mule**: Use tequila instead of vodka.
- **Dark 'N' Stormy**: Use dark rum instead of vodka.

### Tips
- The copper mug keeps the drink extra cold.
- Use quality ginger beer with real ginger for the best flavor.
- Some people prefer more lime; adjust to taste.

## Espresso Martini

The Espresso Martini was created in London in the 1980s by bartender Dick Bradsell when a customer asked for something to "wake me up and then mess me up."

### Ingredients
- 2 oz vodka
- 1 oz coffee liqueur (Kahlúa)
- 1 oz fresh espresso (cooled)
- 1/2 oz simple syrup
- 3 coffee beans for garnish

### Instructions
1. Pull a shot of espresso and let it cool slightly.
2. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
3. Add vodka, coffee liqueur, espresso, and simple syrup.
4. Shake vigorously for 20-30 seconds (this creates the signature foam).
5. Strain into a chilled martini glass.
6. Garnish with three coffee beans.

### Variations
- **Salted Caramel Espresso Martini**: Add salted caramel syrup.
- **Irish Espresso Martini**: Add a splash of Irish cream.
- **Vanilla Espresso Martini**: Use vanilla vodka.

### Tips
- Fresh espresso creates better foam than cold brew.
- The three coffee beans traditionally represent health, wealth, and happiness.
- Shake hard to create the signature frothy top.

## Whiskey Sour

The Whiskey Sour dates back to the 1860s and remains one of the most balanced and refreshing whiskey cocktails.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz bourbon whiskey
- 3/4 oz fresh lemon juice
- 1/2 oz simple syrup
- 1/2 oz egg white (optional, for foam)
- Angostura bitters for garnish
- Lemon wheel and cherry for garnish

### Instructions
1. If using egg white, add all ingredients to a shaker WITHOUT ice first.
2. Dry shake (without ice) for 15 seconds to emulsify the egg white.
3. Add ice and shake again for 15-20 seconds.
4. Strain into a rocks glass over fresh ice.
5. Add a few drops of Angostura bitters on top.
6. Garnish with a lemon wheel and cherry.

### Variations
- **New York Sour**: Float red wine on top.
- **Boston Sour**: The version with egg white.
- **Amaretto Sour**: Use amaretto instead of whiskey.

### Tips
- Egg white is optional but creates a silky texture and beautiful foam.
- The dry shake is essential when using egg white.
- Use aquafaba (chickpea water) as a vegan egg white substitute.

## Daiquiri

The Daiquiri was invented in Cuba in the late 1800s. Despite often being served frozen at beach bars, the classic daiquiri is a simple, elegant cocktail.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz white rum
- 1 oz fresh lime juice
- 3/4 oz simple syrup

### Instructions
1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
2. Add rum, lime juice, and simple syrup.
3. Shake vigorously for 15-20 seconds.
4. Strain into a chilled coupe glass.
5. Optionally garnish with a lime wheel.

### Variations
- **Hemingway Daiquiri**: Add maraschino liqueur and grapefruit juice, reduce sugar.
- **Strawberry Daiquiri**: Blend with fresh strawberries.
- **Banana Daiquiri**: Blend with fresh banana.

### Tips
- The classic daiquiri is served up, not frozen.
- Balance is key: too sweet or too sour ruins the drink.
- Quality rum is essential since it's the star.

## Negroni

The Negroni was invented in Florence, Italy in 1919 when Count Camillo Negroni asked his bartender to strengthen his Americano by replacing the soda water with gin.

### Ingredients
- 1 oz gin
- 1 oz Campari
- 1 oz sweet vermouth
- Orange peel for garnish

### Instructions
1. Add all ingredients to a mixing glass with ice.
2. Stir for 30 seconds until well chilled.
3. Strain into a rocks glass over a large ice cube.
4. Express the oil from an orange peel over the drink and drop it in.

### Variations
- **Boulevardier**: Use bourbon instead of gin.
- **Negroni Sbagliato**: Use prosecco instead of gin.
- **White Negroni**: Use Suze and Lillet Blanc for a lighter version.

### Tips
- The equal parts ratio is traditional, but adjust to taste.
- Use quality sweet vermouth; it makes a difference.
- Stirring (not shaking) keeps the drink clear and silky.

## Cosmopolitan

The Cosmopolitan became famous in the 1990s, partly due to its frequent appearance on Sex and the City. It's a sophisticated, slightly tart cocktail.

### Ingredients
- 1.5 oz citrus vodka
- 1 oz Cointreau
- 1/2 oz fresh lime juice
- 1 oz cranberry juice
- Lime wheel or orange peel for garnish

### Instructions
1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
2. Add vodka, Cointreau, lime juice, and cranberry juice.
3. Shake vigorously for 15-20 seconds.
4. Strain into a chilled martini glass.
5. Garnish with a lime wheel or orange peel.

### Variations
- **White Cosmopolitan**: Use white cranberry juice.
- **Elderflower Cosmo**: Add elderflower liqueur.
- **Blood Orange Cosmo**: Use blood orange juice instead of cranberry.

### Tips
- Use 100% cranberry juice, not cranberry cocktail.
- The drink should be pink, not red (don't use too much cranberry).
- Citron vodka is traditional, but regular vodka works too.

## Gin and Tonic

The Gin and Tonic originated as a medicinal drink in colonial India, where British soldiers mixed their daily quinine (in tonic water) with gin to make it more palatable.

### Ingredients
- 2 oz gin
- 4-5 oz tonic water
- Lime wedge for garnish

### Instructions
1. Fill a highball glass with ice.
2. Pour gin over the ice.
3. Top with tonic water.
4. Gently stir once or twice.
5. Squeeze a lime wedge over the drink and drop it in.

### Variations
- **Spanish G&T**: Served in a large balloon glass with botanical garnishes.
- **Elderflower G&T**: Add elderflower liqueur.
- **Cucumber G&T**: Garnish with cucumber slices.

### Tips
- Use quality tonic water; it's half the drink.
- London Dry gin is classic, but experiment with different styles.
- Don't stir too much; you'll lose the carbonation.
